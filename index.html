Pet Ownership Worldwide

Did you know?

According to the World Pet Association (WPA), there are over 470 million pet dogs and 370 million pet cats worldwide. Dogs and cats are the most popular pets, but other popular pets include fish, birds, rabbits, and hamsters.

The United States has the highest number of pet dogs and cats, with 89.7 million dogs and 94.2 million cats. Other countries with high numbers of pet dogs and cats include China, Brazil, Russia, and India. Pet ownership is on the rise in many parts of the world, including developing countries where the middle class is growing and more people are able to afford pets.

Here are some ways that pets can benefit mental health:

1.Reducing stress and anxiety: Spending time with pets has been shown to reduce stress and anxiety levels, with studies showing that pet owners have lower levels of cortisol, a hormone associated with stress.

2.Providing companionship: Pets provide companionship and can help reduce feelings of loneliness and isolation, particularly for individuals who live alone.

3.Improving mood: Petting or playing with pets has been shown to release endorphins, which can improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression.

4.Encouraging social interaction: Owning a pet can encourage social interaction with other pet owners, whether it's at the dog park or at social events for pet owners

However, all these benefits comes with responsibilities, including providing proper care and attention to pets, ensuring that they are safe and healthy, and addressing any behavioral or health issues promptly. Responsible pet ownership is a lifetime commitment, and pet owners should be prepared to care for their pets for the duration of their lives.

About Pet Abandonment and Pet Abuse

Pet Abandonment

Pet abandonment is a serious issue that affects millions of animals around the world. Whether it's due to financial hardship, a move to a new home, or simply a lack of responsibility, pets are often left to fend for themselves. Abandoned pets face a host of dangers, including starvation, exposure to the elements, and attacks from other animals. It is important for pet owners to take responsibility for their pets and ensure they are well-cared for, or to surrender them to a shelter or rescue organization if they can no longer care for them.


Pet abandonment not only puts animals at risk of physical harm, but also emotional trauma. The sudden loss of a caregiver and familiar surroundings can be distressing for pets, causing them to suffer from anxiety and depression. This issue can be prevented by educating pet owners on the importance of responsible pet ownership and providing resources such as affordable spay/neuter services and pet-friendly housing options.

Pet Abuse

Pet abuse is a cruel and inhumane act that involves intentionally harming or neglecting an animal. This can include physical abuse, such as beating or kicking, or neglecting to provide basic needs like food, water, and medical care. Animals who are abused can suffer from physical injuries, emotional trauma, and even death. It is important for people to report suspected cases of pet abuse to the authorities and to advocate for stricter laws and penalties for those who commit these crimes. Every animal deserves to be treated with compassion and respect.


In addition to physical harm, pet abuse can also have long-lasting effects on an animal's mental well-being. Animals who have been abused may develop trust issues and fear of humans, making it difficult for them to form healthy relationships with future owners.

What Happens After?

There are many organizations around the world that work to help rehome pets and find them new homes. These organizations often work to rescue and rehabilitate animals, provide them with veterinary care, and find them new homes with loving families. They may also offer education and outreach programs to promote responsible pet ownership and animal welfare.

Click here to watch a video made by SPCA

Unfortunately, there are also cases where pets are unable to be rehomed and are euthanized due to health or behavioral issues, or due to overcrowding in animal shelters. This can be a difficult and heartbreaking decision for both pet owners and animal welfare organizations.


To address this issue, some organizations work to reduce pet overpopulation through spay and neuter programs, and others focus on finding innovative solutions for rehoming pets, such as foster programs or partnerships with businesses that allow pets in the workplace. By working together, these organizations can help reduce the number of pets that are euthanized each year, and ensure that all pets have the chance to find loving homes.
